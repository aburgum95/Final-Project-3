Angel Flight Soars
Non-Profit Website Redesign UX/UI Case Study
3/21/22 - 4/09/22
High Level

Project Overview

Angel Flight Soars, a non-profit organization, had a website that needed improvement of their user experience. My team discovered key issues and developed solutions through UI components and structuring. The result was a website redesign that is user-friendly.

The Problem

The current UI Design is outdated, leading users to be confused and turned away from utilizing Angel Flight Soars.

Solution

Apply UI components, restructure the menu navigation, and redesign the website to make it more user-friendly.

My Role

UX Researcher and UI Designer

Design Tools

Adobe XD, Invision, Adobe Color, Miro, Trello and Excel

The Challenge

During the initial meeting with Angel Flight Soars, we gained insight on their goals for both their company as a whole and their website. We discovered some major pain points on the website that were causing their goals with their company to not align with their goals on their website. We discovered there were some major pain points on their website that created difficult navigation.

We recognized this website needed to be user-friendly but only had 2 weeks to achieve this so we quicly set out to develop solutions through UI components, restructuring navigation, and redesigning the website. After implementing our solutions, we conducted 5 usability tests.

Case Study Navigation

Discovery

As human-centric designers, we set out to understand our users & stakeholders.

Stakeholder Interview

After some difficulties of gaining a contact, we were able to meet with Stephen Jamieson, the Project Coordinator.

Key Takeaways:

  • Angel Flight Soar target audiences are patients, pilots and donors
  • They hoped their website was more simplified and easily accessible to patients and pilots. In 3 words: "Professional, easy to navigate, functional
  • Functionalities include patients be able to log into an account, pilots to apply and signup for missions, and donations be received

View The Creative Brief

Usability Analysis

After analyzing the stakeholder interview, I put together an interview script to gain further understanding about the needs of the targeted audience to discover if the organization and their website was meeting this need.

We interviewed a variety of people from all walks of life who would have different medical needs; elders, parents, and single young adults. We took the information from the gathered results to create an affinity diagram to recognize the collective answers and issues.

From the affinity diagram, we discovered the iggest issues were that the website appears outdated, the qiuck links created confusion, and the site did not provide clear information for patients and pilots.

Competitor Analysis and Heuristic Evaluation

We conducted a heuristic evaluation to find the general and obvoius pain points with the website. Through an onboarding competitive analysis, we were able to compare Angel Flight Soars to similar organizations and recognize some methods we could apply to their website but make it even better.

Idenitfy

Through developing user personas, we considered the User Journey our users would take as a first-time visitor to the Angel Flight Soars website.

User Personas

Through our analysis, I was able to develop two user personas based on two key users that visit the Angel Flight Soars website; a certified nurse and patient. However, we recognize there was the need for a pilot persona as well. Unfortunately, time restraints did not allow for this but we still tried to include their path for that key user.

UX Scenario & Storyboard

Understanding the user’s experience that lead up to them visiting the site and throughout their journey allowed us to understand their mindset and goals. We were then able to find the best paths to efficiently meet their needs.

User Flow Diagrams

We developed two main user flows for our user personas to quickly deliver the information they needed and allow them to easily navigate to the next step. We ultimately provided 4 important steps. The homepage, the About Us to help them understand what Angel Flight Soars achieves, select if they’re on the patient side or a pilot.

New Information Hierarchy (AKA Sitemap)

We reviewed the current sitemap and found some pain points that could use some improvement. This included pages that didn’t allow the user easily navigate back to the homepage and pages that didn’t have the same quick links. We developed a new hierarchy that was better organized for the user expectation. This included a top navigation and bottom navigation in the header so the user could get to any page they needed regardless of where they were at on the site.

Design

After crafting our user persona, considered the User Journey that a frist-time visitor would take, we set out our journey in design.

Site Style Guide

The goal with the style guide was to create a professional, clean website that honored their color scheme. We included the color, typography, iconography, and controls.

Templated Sections

Since we were limited in time, we produced templated sections for the team to use to rapidly produce a hi-fidelity prototype for a cohesive experience across the website.

Verify

After designing, we set out to verify our decisions.

Prototyping & Testing

Our hi-fidelity testing revealed that our users were able to complete the intended user flows with no issues.

Key Takeaways:

  • Users were able to complete tasks easily
  • Make the elements in the primary navigation and footer more visible
  • Align elements in a grid to provide a cleaner responsive design between mobile and desktop screen sizes

Updated Design

We delivered a moder, professional design that features the functionality and accessibility that meets both the organization and user’s needs.

View Our Hi-Fidelity Prototype

Final Thoughts

The final product is professional, clean, and modern. Now we propel Angle Flight Soars' online presence into the future.

Results

Our redesign came at the pefect time, as Angel Flight Soars was preparing to update their online presence. We have sent our design to the organization and patiently wait to see what elements will be included in their final product.

Our hope with this redesign is to give medical patients and pilots a sense of empowerment and security when working with such a great organization. We expect more website traffic, with greater user retention rates and delightful experience when researching Angel Flight Soars online, all in the aim of increasing awareness and reaching those in need of their services.
